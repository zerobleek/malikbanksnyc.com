Summary

MomKnows! collects nothing about you. No analytics, no crash reporters, no accounts, no third-party SDKs, and no outbound network traffic from the app. Everything you put into MomKnows! stays on your device. The only network activity during your lifetime with the app is Apple's purchase flow when you buy the app, which Apple runs — we do not observe, record, or transmit anything about it. This policy explains that posture in full.

Who we are

MomKnows! is published by Bleek Vision LLC, a New Jersey limited liability company. The app is built and operated by Shabaka Malik Banks. For any question about this policy or the app's privacy posture, email malikbanksnyc@gmail.com.

What this policy covers

This policy applies to the MomKnows! iOS application, distributed through the Apple App Store and supported on iOS 17 and later. It does not cover Apple's App Store, StoreKit, iCloud, or any other Apple service — those are governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. It also does not cover any other Bleek Vision LLC product; each product has its own policy.

What we do not collect

MomKnows! is designed so that Bleek Vision LLC never comes into possession of your information. Specifically, the app does not collect or transmit:

  • Personal information of any kind — no name, email, phone number, address, or other identifier you provide to us.
  • Account credentials — the app has no accounts and no login.
  • Device identifiers — no advertising identifier (IDFA), no vendor identifier, no fingerprinting.
  • Usage analytics — no sessions, no screen views, no feature-usage telemetry, and no first-party or third-party analytics SDK is embedded.
  • Crash reports — no crash reporter is embedded. If the app crashes, the report exists only in Apple's per-device crash logs, which you may choose to share with Apple; we receive nothing from that channel.
  • Location — the app does not request or use location services.
  • Contacts, calendar, reminders, or any other system data that would require explicit permission beyond the specific permissions listed below.
  • Network traffic from the app itself — the app makes no outbound network requests to Bleek Vision LLC, to any third party, or to any analytics, logging, advertising, or marketing service. This is an architectural fact verifiable with any network inspector.

The App Store privacy label for MomKnows! is "Data Not Collected" across every category defined by Apple. That label is accurate.

One narrow exception applies: when you purchase the app, your device communicates with Apple's StoreKit service to process the transaction. That purchase flow is operated entirely by Apple. MomKnows! does not observe, record, or transmit anything about your purchase, and Bleek Vision LLC receives only the aggregate, anonymized sales reports that Apple provides to every App Store developer. We never see your payment method, your Apple Account, or your identity.

What the app stores on your device

Everything you enter into MomKnows! — family members' names, medications, contacts, stories, photos, videos, audio recordings, and any other content you capture — is stored locally on your iPhone or iPad. The app uses two on-device stores:

  • Local SwiftData storage for structured content (the Circle of people you are preserving information for, the answers you capture, and the stories you record).
  • The iOS Keychain for sensitive individual fields such as insurance policy numbers. Keychain items are stored with the accessibility class kSecAttrAccessibleWhenUnlockedThisDeviceOnly, which means they are bound to your specific device and do not sync through iCloud Keychain.

Retention. Your data remains on your device for as long as you keep MomKnows! installed.

Deletion. Deleting the app from your device removes all data it has stored locally. If you have created an encrypted backup (see below) and saved it outside the app, deleting the app will not automatically delete that file — it remains wherever you saved it until you delete it yourself.

Permissions the app requests

MomKnows! asks for the following iOS permissions only at the moment you try to use the related feature. You can decline any of them and still use the rest of the app.

  • Face ID / Touch ID. Used to unlock the app if you enable biometric lock. Biometric authentication is handled by Apple's LocalAuthentication framework; your biometric template never leaves Apple's Secure Enclave and is never shared with the app or with us.
  • Microphone. Used when you record audio directly inside the app, or when you use dictation to transcribe a longer answer.
  • Speech Recognition. Used for on-device dictation via Apple's Speech framework. The app uses the requiresOnDeviceRecognition = true setting, meaning audio never leaves your device for recognition. If your device or locale does not support on-device recognition, dictation is disabled — the app does not fall back to cloud-based recognition.
  • Camera. Used when you capture a photo or video directly inside the app.
  • Photo Library. Used when you attach an existing photo or video from your library.

Encryption and on-device security

MomKnows! uses Apple frameworks exclusively for all cryptography. No third-party cryptographic libraries are embedded.

  • Encrypted backup files are encrypted with AES-256-GCM via Apple's CryptoKit framework.
  • Key derivation for the backup passphrase uses PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 via Apple's CommonCrypto, with 100,000 iterations and a 16-byte random salt generated per backup.
  • Keychain storage for sensitive fields uses the accessibility class described above; these items are device-bound and do not sync.
  • Biometric unlock uses Apple's LocalAuthentication framework. Your biometric data never leaves the Secure Enclave.

Backups you create

MomKnows! lets you create an encrypted backup file of your data. You choose the passphrase. The passphrase is never stored inside the app, never transmitted anywhere, and is not recoverable by Bleek Vision LLC — not by technical means, not by request. If you lose the passphrase, your backup cannot be decrypted. That is a deliberate design choice: it is the mechanism that keeps your backup file unreadable to anyone who is not you, including us.

When you export the backup file, you choose where it goes — iCloud Drive, a USB-C drive, another Files provider, or anywhere else iOS's document picker can reach. The app writes the file at your explicit instruction using iOS security-scoped bookmarks. No cloud service of ours is involved.

Apple's role

Your copy of MomKnows! is delivered to you through the Apple App Store, and your purchase is processed by Apple's StoreKit. Optional iCloud features you may use on your device — for example, saving a backup file to iCloud Drive — are operated by Apple. Your relationship with Apple is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy and Apple's terms, not by this policy.

Children

MomKnows! is rated 17+ on the App Store and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13 — or, for that matter, from any user at all. If you believe a child under 13 has used the app, note that because no data is transmitted to Bleek Vision LLC, no data about that child exists on our side to delete. To remove the child's locally-stored content, delete the app from the device.

Your rights

Privacy laws in the European Union (GDPR), the United Kingdom (UK GDPR), and California (CCPA / CPRA) give you rights regarding personal data that a company holds about you — including the right to access, correct, delete, and receive a portable copy of that data.

Those rights are satisfied in the case of MomKnows! because Bleek Vision LLC holds no personal data about you. There is nothing to access, correct, delete, or transfer on our side, because nothing was ever transmitted to us. The data you care about lives on your device, under your control:

  • Access your data by opening the app.
  • Correct your data by editing it inside the app.
  • Delete your data by deleting individual entries, or by deleting the app, which removes all locally-stored data.
  • Portability. Use the app's export features (PDF, Notes, or the encrypted backup file) to move your data wherever you choose.

If you believe we hold data about you and wish to exercise any of these rights against Bleek Vision LLC directly, email malikbanksnyc@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days. Our honest answer will, in every case we can currently imagine, be that we hold no such data.

Bleek Vision LLC does not meet the regulatory thresholds requiring a Data Protection Officer and does not have one. The email address above reaches Shabaka Malik Banks directly.
