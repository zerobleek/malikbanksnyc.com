Why MomKnows

The knowledge you need
lives in one head.

It's the ER intake desk asking which medication your mother takes, and you don't know. It's the 2 a.m. phone call from a sibling who can't find the Medicare card. It's a bad diagnosis, a hospital bed, an estate to sort, a will nobody can locate. The knowledge that becomes urgent in those moments — which doctor, which hospital, which lawyer, which bank, who to call first, where the documents are — usually exists in exactly one place. In a parent's head.

It evaporates the moment you need it. Not because anyone hid it. Because nobody wrote it down, and the conversation that would have written it down is the one nobody wants to have.

MomKnows! is a structured, private way to have that conversation now, while it's still a conversation and not a crisis. It works by category, in plain language, at the pace of a Sunday afternoon. The stories are welcome — the recipes, the family history, the things that aren't on any form. But the primary work is operational, and the primary user is the adult child who already knows what it feels like to wish they had asked.

Voice & Stories

Speak it.
It stays on the phone.

Every long-answer field accepts spoken input. Dictation is transcribed entirely on the device using Apple's Speech framework, with on-device recognition required. Audio never leaves the phone — there is no fallback to cloud recognition. If your device or language doesn't support on-device transcription, dictation is disabled rather than rerouted. A deliberate refusal, not an oversight.

Stories is the open-ended counterpart to the guided questions. Voice memories, one-off anecdotes, the family recipe in the recorder's actual voice, the inherited piece of advice you don't want to lose the cadence of. Photos, videos, and audio recordings can ride along with any story. Stored locally — or on an external USB-C drive of your choice — and bound to whichever Circle profile they belong to.

Most stories begin as a question you didn't know you were going to ask. Start the recording, let the person talk, and the transcript builds itself in plain text you can edit later. The audio file stays alongside the transcript, so the voice is preserved even after the words are tidied up.

Privacy as architecture

Not a posture.
A property of the build.

MomKnows! has no accounts, no servers, no cloud sync, no iCloud sync, no third-party backends, no analytics, no crash reporters, and no tracking SDKs of any kind. The App Store privacy label reads "Data Not Collected" for every category Apple defines — and that is verifiable with any network inspector you'd care to point at the app, because the only outbound traffic the app produces is Apple's own StoreKit purchase flow, which is handled by iOS rather than by us.

Sensitive fields — insurance numbers, account numbers, the kinds of details you wouldn't paste into a notes app — are stored in the iOS Keychain with the WhenUnlockedThisDeviceOnly accessibility class. Device-bound. Not synced through iCloud Keychain. Not pulled into a backup unless you explicitly create one. The whole app sits behind biometric unlock (Face ID / Touch ID with a PIN fallback) so a phone left on a kitchen counter is not a phone with the family's medical history on it.

When you create an encrypted backup, the file is sealed with AES-256-GCM using a key derived from your passphrase via PBKDF2-HMAC-SHA256 with 100,000 iterations and a fresh 16-byte salt. You choose the passphrase. We never see it, never store it, and cannot recover it. That is not a policy promise — it is the only configuration that keeps the file unreadable to anyone who is not you. Export the backup anywhere iOS can write a file. Restore it on any iPhone. Take your data with you whenever you decide to go.

FAQ

The questions buyers ask.

Is my data sent to your servers?

No — and the reason is structural rather than promised. Bleek Vision LLC operates no servers for MomKnows!. The app makes no outbound network requests of its own. The App Store privacy label reads "Data Not Collected" because nothing is collected.

What happens if I lose my phone?

If you've created an encrypted backup and saved it somewhere — iCloud Drive, a USB-C drive, your computer — you can restore it onto a new iPhone using your passphrase. There is no cloud recovery from us, because there is no cloud.

What happens if I lose my backup passphrase?

Your backup cannot be decrypted. Not by us, not by appeal, not by any technical means. That's not us being unhelpful — it's the only configuration that keeps the file unreadable to anyone who is not you. Choose a passphrase you can recover, and write it somewhere safe.

Does this sync between my iPhone and iPad?

Not at launch. Each device holds its own copy. The encrypted backup file is the bridge between devices today.

Can I share a Circle member with my sibling?

Not at launch. Sharing is on the roadmap, but only as a feature designed without weakening the privacy architecture. In the meantime, the Emergency Packet PDF is the easiest way to get the most important pieces to a sibling who isn't using the app.

Why isn't this on Android?

iOS only at launch. No Android plans announced. The privacy guarantees lean heavily on specific Apple frameworks (Keychain, Secure Enclave, on-device Speech) and we'd rather build one platform with integrity than two with caveats.

Is this a subscription?

No. One-time purchase, $29 as an intro price for a limited time, then $49.99. Future updates included.

Is this medical or legal advice?

No. MomKnows! is a personal record-keeping tool. It captures what you and your family already know, in a structured place. It is not a substitute for medical records, legal documents, or professional advice — verify critical information with the doctors, lawyers, and advisors who have actually been retained for it.

How do I delete my data?

Delete the app. That removes everything stored locally, including the Keychain entries the app created. Backup files you exported elsewhere are yours to delete on your own terms.
